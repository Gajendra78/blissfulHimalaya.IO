Aum Pravat (ॐ) Trek

(13 Days / 12 Night)

Previous Next

According to the peoples, there are only eight mountains in India, which has an impression of AUM symbol (ॐ). Out of these eight, only one has been discovered, which is known as Om Parvat or Adi Kailash. This magical mountain, Adi Kailash, is standing tall at an elevation of 6191 m in the Dharchula Pithorgarh district. It is quite known for its strong religious and adventure connect, also, it is believed that the mountain has a miraculous nature. this place is considered to be one of the oldest abode of Lord Shiva. Related to the legend of Ravana, the peak is considered sacred by many, and the Om Parvat trek leads to an astounding view of the giant and the impression of '(ॐ)' made on the facade by the snow.

    The journey to Aum Parvat (ॐ) is much more than just a trek. It hosts a vast collection of blessing ,mythological folklore around it that blur the line between myth and reality. And all of it is waiting out there to be explored.

Itinerary

Start & End Point -: Haldwani ( Or as you wish)

Day 01 :
Drive Haldwani To Askote (250 Km by road):

Day 02 :
Askote To Mangati then trek to Gala.

Day 03 :
Trek to Budhi 18 kms trek.

Day 04 :
Trek to Gunji 19 kms trek/5-6 hrs.

Day 05 :
Trek from Gunji to Kutti (20 Kms by trek).

Day 06 :
Trek from Kutti to Jolingkong (14 km by trek).

Day 07:
Trek from Jolingkong to Adi Kailash/Parvati Sarovar and back to Jolingkong and Kutti (21 km by trek).

Day 08:
Trek from Kutti to Gunji (20 km by trek) .

Day 09:
Trekking from Gunji to Nabhidang via Kalapani (18 km by trek).

Day 10:
Trekking from Nabhidang to Gunji via Kalapani (18 km by trek).

Day 11:
Trekking from Gunji to Budhi (19 km by trek).

Day 12:
Trek from Budhi to Mangati to Askote.

Day 13:
Askote to Haldwani.
